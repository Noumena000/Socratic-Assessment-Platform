The problem

Finished answers alone no longer tell instructors enough about understanding.

Polished responses can hide whether a learner can actually explain a concept, defend it under pressure, apply it in a new case, or revise it when challenged.

Socratic Assessment does not try to detect AI use. It creates an assessment setting where understanding must become visible through dialogue and where instructors can review evidence rather than rely on surface fluency.

What Socratic Assessment Does

Assessment is treated as an evidence-building process, not simple answer checking.

The platform helps instructors see how a learner defines terms, connects ideas, responds to questions, revises a claim, and supports it with reasons. The result is a structured record that can inform human judgment.

Dialogue-Guided Assessment

Questioning moves the learner beyond a prepared answer and into explanation, distinction, application, and revision.

Evidence Accumulation

Each turn can add support, reveal uncertainty, or expose misconceptions that matter for review.

Reasoning Visibility

The interface highlights how an answer changes over time rather than treating the first response as final.

Instructor Judgment

The system prepares a decision snapshot for faculty. It does not replace evaluation, and it does not automatically grade learners.

How the Reasoning Architecture Works

A visible assessment workflow backed by a deeper reasoning process.

Privacy + Faculty Control

Evidence supports instructor judgment. It does not replace it.

Faculty remain responsible for evaluation. The platform is designed to support instructor judgment, not automate it away.

No automatic grading claim. The public assessment experience does not present machine-generated scores as final academic decisions.

Not an AI detector. The platform examines reasoning through dialogue instead of making claims about authorship.

Evidence over verdicts. Dialogue produces reviewable evidence intended to help instructors make better assessment decisions.

Institutional caution. Any real deployment would require approved governance, secure data handling, accessibility review, and faculty oversight.
