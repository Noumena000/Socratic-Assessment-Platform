The instructional problem

Traditional assessment often captures conclusions more clearly than reasoning.

Many student submissions show what a learner concluded, but not how the learner handled ambiguity, objections, evidence, conceptual distinctions, or revision.

This prototype explores whether structured AI-assisted dialogue can produce formative evidence of understanding while keeping instructors in control of instructional decisions.

Privacy and governance

Built for a cautious institutional conversation.

Public showcase: static, fictional, and non-operational.

Private development: reasoning engine, scoring logic, prompt architecture, and API integration remain private.

Production requirement: institutionally approved AI provider, secure backend, FERPA-aware data handling, retention rules, accessibility review, and faculty oversight.

Roadmap

From concept prototype to controlled pilot

Phase A

Static showcase and fictional examples for feedback.

Phase B

Secure live LLM prototype with no real student records.

Phase C

Faculty-reviewed formative reports and rubric alignment.

Phase D

Institutional pilot after privacy, accessibility, and governance review.

Design Principles

Five principles guide the platform

  • AI supports faculty—it does not replace faculty.
  • Assessment should examine reasoning, not only answers.
  • Student privacy comes before automation.
  • Transparency matters more than prediction.
  • Human judgment remains central.

Meeting framing

“I am not proposing deployment yet. I am looking for strategic feedback on whether this direction could support formative assessment, where the governance risks are, and what would be required before any institutional pilot.”
