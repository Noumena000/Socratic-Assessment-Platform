Public example

1. Submitted paper

In healthcare, autonomy is a critical principle. Patients have the right to make treatment decisions, but autonomy must be balanced with beneficence and an informed understanding of consequences.

AutonomyBeneficenceInformed choice

2. Socratic dialogue

System: Define autonomy as you use it in the paper.
Learner: It is the patient’s authority to make an informed treatment decision.
System: When might autonomy conflict with beneficence?
Learner: When a patient refuses a beneficial treatment, though the provider must still respect an informed choice.

3. Understanding map

4. Faculty snapshot

Observed strength: the learner clearly distinguishes autonomous choice from provider preference.

Remaining question: can the learner apply the distinction when decision-making capacity is uncertain?

Faculty action: review the transcript and request one transfer case before final evaluation.

Academic integrity by design

The strongest safeguard is accountable reasoning—not a fragile lockout.

Session controls may support assessment conditions, but the platform’s real value is requiring learners to explain and revise ideas in ways that create inspectable evidence.

01

Explanation over detection

The platform does not claim to determine who authored a paper. It tests whether the learner can explain the ideas attributed to them.

02

Evidence over verdicts

Inconsistencies, revisions, and transfer attempts are surfaced as evidence for faculty—not converted into automatic misconduct accusations.

03

Faculty over automation

Human instructors remain responsible for academic judgment, contextual interpretation, and any consequential decision.

Privacy + faculty control

Evidence supports instructor judgment. It does not replace it.

No automatic grading claim. Machine-generated signals are not presented as final academic decisions.

Not an AI detector. The system evaluates demonstrated reasoning rather than making authorship claims.

Accessible review. Faculty receive a concise transcript, evidence map, and suggested follow-up—not opaque scores.

Protected architecture. This public layer demonstrates the experience without exposing private prompts, code, or institutional data.
