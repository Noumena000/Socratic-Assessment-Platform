The instructional problem

AI-written work makes it harder to infer understanding from finished answers alone.

Students can now arrive with polished essays, quiz answers, or discussion posts without necessarily being able to explain the concepts behind them.

This prototype does not try to “detect AI.” It creates a setting where understanding must survive questioning: definition, distinction, application, objection, and revision.

Understanding through dialogue

Copy-paste is insufficient when a learner must reason in real time.

The platform treats a prepared answer as the beginning of assessment, not the end. A learner demonstrates understanding only when they can explain the claim, connect it to course concepts, respond to pressure, and revise thoughtfully.

Recommended LM Studio settings

  1. Open LM Studio and start the local server.
  2. Use an OpenAI-compatible chat completions endpoint.
  3. Keep the endpoint as http://127.0.0.1:1234/v1/chat/completions unless LM Studio shows a different port.

Design Principles

Five principles guide the platform

  • AI supports faculty—it does not replace faculty.
  • Assessment should examine reasoning, not only answers.
  • Student privacy comes before automation.
  • Transparency matters more than prediction.
  • Human judgment remains central.

Meeting framing

“I am not proposing deployment yet. I am exploring whether structured dialogue can help faculty gather better evidence of student understanding, especially in an era when polished written answers may not reliably show what a learner can explain, defend, or revise.”
